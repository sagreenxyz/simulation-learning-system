## 🔑 The Big Picture — NRP Algorithm

```
Newborn delivered
        ↓
Initial Assessment (first 30 seconds):
  • Term gestation? Good tone? Breathing/Crying?
        ↓
If YES all three → Routine care (warm, dry, stimulate, skin-to-skin)
        ↓
If NO to any → Begin resuscitation:
        ↓
STEP 1 (0–30 sec): Warm → Dry and stimulate → Clear airway (suction if meconium) → Reposition
        ↓
STEP 2 (30 sec): Assess: Breathing? HR?
  • HR ≥ 100 + breathing → Monitor; SpO2 probe
  • HR < 100 OR apnea → PPV with room air or O2 (21%)
        ↓
STEP 3 (60 sec): PPV → Assess HR
  • HR ≥ 100 → Continue monitoring
  • HR 60–100 → Continue PPV; check ventilation (MR SOPA)
  • HR < 60 → Begin chest compressions + PPV (100% O2)
        ↓
STEP 4: HR < 60 despite compressions → Epinephrine; consider intubation
```

## 📝 PRE-SIMULATION QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Q1. What are the initial steps of newborn assessment at birth?

**The Three Questions (in first 30 seconds):**
1. Is the baby **term** (≥ 37 weeks)?
2. Does the baby have **good muscle tone**?
3. Is the baby **breathing or crying**?

**If YES to all three:** Routine newborn care — warm, dry, stimulate, skin-to-skin with mother

**If NO to any one:** Begin initial steps of resuscitation:
- **Warm** — prevent hypothermia (critical in neonates; hypothermia worsens outcomes)
- **Dry and stimulate** — vigorous drying stimulates breathing; dry thoroughly
- **Reposition** — sniffing position (slight neck extension) to open airway
- **Clear airway** — suction mouth first, then nose (M before N)
- **Assess** — breathing, heart rate (by auscultation or feel umbilical pulse)

---

### Q2. How is the neonatal heart rate assessed and what are the critical thresholds?

**Assessment method:** Auscultate with stethoscope at left sternal border OR palpate base of umbilical cord (umbilical pulse); count 6 seconds × 10 = beats per minute

**Critical thresholds:**
| HR | Action |
|---|---|
| **≥ 100 bpm** | Normal; continue monitoring and routine care if breathing |
| **60–100 bpm** | Slow; initiate or continue PPV |
| **< 60 bpm** | Critical; begin CHEST COMPRESSIONS + PPV with 100% O2 |
| **< 60 despite compressions** | Medications (epinephrine) |

---

### Q3. What is positive pressure ventilation (PPV) and when is it initiated?

**Initiation criteria (any one):**
- Apnea or gasping
- HR < 100 bpm
- Persistent central cyanosis despite free-flow O2

**Technique:**
1. Position: sniffing position
2. Select appropriate mask size (covers nose and mouth, not eyes or chin)
3. Seal mask firmly on face
4. Squeeze bag — observe for **chest rise** (primary indicator of effective ventilation)
5. Rate: **40–60 breaths per minute** ("breathe — two — three, breathe — two — three")
6. Pressure: **20–25 cmH2O** (higher for first breath, up to 30–40 cmH2O)
7. Start with **21% oxygen** (room air) in term newborns
8. **SpO2 target by minute of life:**
   - 1 min: 60–65%
   - 2 min: 65–70%
   - 3 min: 70–75%
   - 4 min: 75–80%
   - 5 min: 80–85%
   - 10 min: 85–95%

**MR SOPA** (corrective steps if no chest rise with PPV):
- **M** — Mask readjustment
- **R** — Reposition airway
- **S** — Suction (mouth, then nose)
- **O** — Open mouth (small jaw thrust)
- **P** — Pressure increase
- **A** — Airway alternative (intubation)

---

### Q4. What are neonatal chest compressions?

**Initiation:** HR < 60 bpm despite 30 seconds of effective PPV

**Technique:**
- **Two-thumb encircling technique** (preferred): both thumbs on lower third of sternum; fingers encircle chest; compress 1/3 anterior-posterior diameter
- Rate: **90 compressions + 30 breaths per minute** = 3:1 ratio (3 compressions : 1 breath)
- Synchronized to allow full chest recoil; do not interrupt PPV
- Use **100% oxygen** when compressions are being performed
- Reassess HR after 60 seconds of compressions

---

### Q5. How does the nurse support the parents during neonatal resuscitation?

- One team member designated to communicate with parents throughout
- **Brief, honest, calm updates:** "Your baby needs a little help breathing. We are helping her right now."
- Avoid alarming language while also not minimizing the situation
- **After stabilization:** Allow parents to see and hold baby as soon as safely possible
- Address questions fully once situation is stabilized
- Document all communications and parental responses

## 🏥 DURING THE SIMULATION — Your Role

As part of the **nursing care team**, you will:

1. **Prepare the warmer** before delivery — turn on, warm blankets ready, equipment checked
2. **Receive newborn** — place on radiant warmer
3. **Dry, stimulate, and reposition** — vigorous drying; discard wet blanket
4. **Clear airway** — suction with bulb syringe if needed (mouth before nose)
5. **Assess: 3 questions** — tone, breathing, HR
6. **Communicate with RN and team** — "Heart rate is [X]. Baby [is/is not] breathing."
7. **Initiate PPV** if indicated — per team direction and NRP protocol
8. **Apply SpO2 probe** — right hand (preductal)
9. **Support parents** — designate one team member for communication
10. **Document** — APGAR scores at 1 and 5 minutes

### APGAR Score
| Parameter | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A**ppearance | Blue/pale all over | Blue extremities, pink body | Pink all over |
| **P**ulse | Absent | < 100 bpm | ≥ 100 bpm |
| **G**rimace | No response | Grimace | Cry, cough, sneeze |
| **A**ctivity | Limp | Some flexion | Active motion |
| **R**espiration | Absent | Weak, irregular | Strong cry |

- 7–10: Normal
- 4–6: Moderate distress — stimulation and O2 needed
- 0–3: Severe — full resuscitation

## 💊 MEDICATION REVIEW

### Epinephrine — Neonatal
| Category | Details |
|---|---|
| **Indication** | HR < 60 despite 30 sec effective compressions + PPV with 100% O2 |
| **Route** | Umbilical venous catheter (IV) preferred; endotracheal (higher dose) if no IV access |
| **Dose** | 0.01–0.03 mg/kg IV; 0.05–0.1 mg/kg ET |
| **Concentration** | 1:10,000 (0.1 mg/mL) |

---

## 🏥 PRIORITY NURSING DIAGNOSES (Newborn)

1. **Impaired Spontaneous Ventilation** r/t respiratory distress at birth — AEB: apnea, HR < 100, poor tone
2. **Risk for Hypothermia** r/t transition from intrauterine environment — AEB: newborn exposed to room temperature; wet
3. **Impaired Gas Exchange** r/t inadequate spontaneous respiration — AEB: cyanosis, low SpO2
4. **Parental Anxiety** r/t unexpected neonatal resuscitation — AEB: unprepared first-time parents witnessing resuscitation
